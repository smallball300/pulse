Three-Phase Structure
I
Phase 1 · Weeks 1–4
Foundation
Rebuild movement quality, reintroduce load, and begin the run/walk protocol. Hip flexor and calf correctives are mandatory in every session. Lifting is full-body 3x/week. Run sessions 2x/week using 1:2 run:walk intervals. Nutrition: begin consistent food scale tracking.
3 days/wk · Low-Moderate Intensity
II
Phase 2 · Weeks 5–9
Build
Increase to 4 training days. Add commercial gym access and heavier compound loading. Run intervals shift to 2:1 and then continuous running by week 8. Lower body emphasis increases. Correctives maintained but become shorter as mobility improves.
4 days/wk · Moderate-High Intensity
III
Phase 3 · Weeks 10–12
Peak
Taper lifting volume by 20%, increase run distance. Week 11 is a full 5K practice run at comfortable pace. Week 12 is your race week — taper run to 2 miles mid-week, rest 2 days out, run your 5K.
3–4 days/wk · Taper + Peak
Training Principles

Run Progression Logic

  • Start: 0.25 mi continuous → build via run/walk intervals
  • Week 1–4: run 1 min, walk 2 min × 8–10 rounds
  • Week 5–7: run 2 min, walk 1 min × 8 rounds
  • Week 8–9: run 8–12 min continuous, walk 2, repeat
  • Week 10–11: run 20–30 min continuous
  • Week 12: taper to 5K race day

Lifting Structure

  • Phase 1: Full-body 3x/wk, 3×10–12, moderate load
  • Phase 2: Upper/Lower split 4x/wk, 4×6–10, progressive
  • Phase 3: Volume –20%, maintain intensity
  • Lower body priority: glutes, hamstrings, calves every session
  • No leg day isolation from runs — manage fatigue via Whoop

Whoop Recovery Logic

  • Green (67–100): Train as programmed, full effort
  • Yellow (34–66): Reduce volume 20%, skip AMRAP sets, easy run pace
  • Red (0–33): Active recovery only — correctives + 20 min walk
  • Two consecutive red days = mandatory full rest

Nutrition Guidance

  • You have the food scale — start tracking protein first
  • Target: 0.8–1g protein per lb of bodyweight
  • Slight caloric deficit (~300 kcal) on non-training days
  • Eat at maintenance on lifting days, slight surplus on long run days
  • Hydration: minimum 80–100oz water/day, more on run days

Mandatory Corrective Protocol — Done Before Every Session (10 min)

You have weak lower body, tight hip flexors, and underdeveloped calves. These three areas will limit both your running economy and lifting performance if not addressed. Do this every session — no exceptions in Phase 1.

Phase Corrective Progression

Phase 1 (Wks 1–4)

All three corrective blocks before every session. Full 10 minutes. Non-negotiable. This is where you build the foundation your runs will depend on.

Phase 2 (Wks 5–9)

Hip flexor and calf blocks remain mandatory pre-session. Lower body stability block moves to post-session or can be embedded as warm-up sets within the lifting session.

Phase 3 (Wks 10–12)

Correctives become maintenance — 5 minutes pre-session focused on whichever area feels most restricted that day. Trust your body signals here.

Red Flag Signals

  • Sharp knee pain during step-ups → stop, run easy only, no squats
  • Anterior hip pain after runs → extra hip flexor work, reduce run volume
  • Calf tightness that doesn't resolve in 48hrs → rest, ice, see a provider
  • Lower back fatigue → dead bug and bird dog priority that week
Coach Notes — Read Before You Start

On Being a Junior Runner

You're not a beginner at training — you're a beginner at running. Those are different. Your cardiovascular ceiling is higher than a true novice, but your connective tissue (tendons, ligaments, plantar fascia) hasn't adapted to impact loading yet.


The run/walk protocol isn't a crutch — it's injury prevention. Most new runners get hurt because they run continuously before their tissue is ready. You will not skip the walk intervals in Phase 1.

On 2 Months Off

Your neuromuscular patterns are mostly intact. You'll feel strength coming back fast in weeks 1–3. Don't chase old PRs in Phase 1 — your tendons and stabilizers lag behind your perceived strength by 2–4 weeks.


Load at 60–70% of what you think your max is in week 1. You'll be back to working weights by week 3 naturally.

On the Equipment Transition

Home gym gets you through Phase 1 fully. By week 5, you'll want barbell squats, Romanian deadlifts, and cable work for the lower body volume this plan requires. The commercial gym at week 5 is on purpose — it's when the program demands it.


Don't buy the membership early just to have it. Use the consistency milestone as the gate.

On Leaning Up While Running

Running burns fewer calories than people expect at slow speeds. Leaning up here comes from lifting intensity + moderate deficit + protein, not mileage.


Hit your protein target first. Everything else — carb timing, meal frequency — is secondary. The food scale habit you're building is the right move.

On Whoop Data

Your HRV baseline matters more than the number. If your baseline HRV is 45ms and you wake up at 38ms with a yellow score, that's a signal. The app's recovery percentage accounts for this — trust the percentage over the raw HRV number.


Two consecutive red days is a hard stop. One red day, you can train easy. Three in a row means something systemic is wrong — sleep, nutrition, or stress.

Race Week Protocol (Wk 12)

Tuesday: 2 mile easy run, nothing more.
Wednesday: Correctives only, 10 min walk.
Thursday: Full rest. No gym.
Friday: Optional 10-min easy walk.
Saturday: 5K. Start at conversational pace. Don't go out fast.


Eat a normal meal 2–3 hours before. No experiments. Sleep 8+ hours Thursday night.
